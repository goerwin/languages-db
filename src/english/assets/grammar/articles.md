---
title: English Articles
---

# a

form: a + singular countable noun (starting with a consonant sound)

use: To introduce something for the first time; when the listener doesn't know which specific one
eg: I saw a dog in the park
eg: She bought a new car last week

use: To talk about one of many; any one, it doesn't matter which
eg: Can I have a glass of water?
eg: Is there a pharmacy near here?

use: With jobs and professions
eg: She is a teacher
eg: He wants to be a doctor

use: To mean "per" or "each"
eg: I go to the gym three times a week
eg: The speed limit is 60 miles an hour

use: With certain expressions (a few, a little, a lot of, what a..., such a...)
eg: I have a few friends in Paris
eg: Could you add a little sugar to my coffee?
eg: There are a lot of people at the concert
eg: What a beautiful day!
eg: She is such a talented musician

tip: Use "a" before consonant SOUNDS (not just consonant letters): a university (/juː/), a European
tip: Never use "a/an" with plural or uncountable nouns

# an

form: an + singular countable noun (starting with a vowel sound)

use: Same rules as "a" but before vowel SOUNDS; to introduce something for the first time
eg: I saw an elephant at the zoo
eg: She bought an umbrella yesterday

use: Same rules as "a" but before vowel SOUNDS; one of many, any one
eg: Can I have an apple?
eg: Is there an Italian restaurant nearby?

use: Same rules as "a" but before vowel SOUNDS; with jobs and professions
eg: He is an engineer
eg: She wants to be an architect

use: Same rules as "a" but before vowel SOUNDS; meaning "per" or "each"
eg: I exercise twice an hour
eg: The car gets 30 miles an gallon

tip: Use "an" before vowel SOUNDS: an hour (silent h), an honest person, an MBA (em-bee-ay)
tip: Use "a" before consonant SOUNDS even with vowel letters: a university (/juː/), a European (/jʊər/), a one-way street (/wʌn/)
tip: The choice depends on SOUND not spelling: an FBI agent (eff-bee-eye), a UFO (you-eff-oh)

# the

form: the + singular or plural noun, countable or uncountable

use: Second mention; when something has already been introduced or is already known
eg: I saw a dog. The dog was running in the park
eg: She bought a car. The car is red

use: Unique things; there is only one in our world or context
eg: The sun rises in the east
eg: The moon is full tonight
eg: The internet has changed how we communicate
eg: The government announced new policies

use: With superlatives
eg: She is the best student in the class
eg: Mount Everest is the tallest mountain in the world
eg: This is the most interesting book I've ever read

use: With ordinal numbers
eg: This is the first time I've been here
eg: She lives on the second floor
eg: The third attempt was successful

use: With certain place names (countries with plural or "of", deserts, oceans, rivers, mountain ranges)
eg: The United States is a large country
eg: The Netherlands is famous for tulips
eg: The Sahara is the largest hot desert
eg: The Pacific Ocean is vast
eg: The Amazon flows through Brazil
eg: The Alps separate Italy from France

use: With musical instruments
eg: She plays the piano beautifully
eg: He learned to play the guitar as a teenager

use: With "same"
eg: We had the same idea
eg: I want the same thing as you

use: Shared knowledge between speaker and listener; both know which one
eg: Pass me the salt please
eg: Have you seen the keys?
eg: I'm going to the bank

use: With adjectives used as nouns to refer to a group
eg: The rich should help the poor
eg: We need to take care of the elderly
eg: The young often disagree with the old

tip: Do not use "the" with most country names: Japan, France, Brazil (exceptions: the USA, the UK, the Netherlands)
tip: Do not use "the" with lakes or single mountains: Lake Superior, Mount Fuji

# zero article (no article)

form: No article before the noun

use: Plural and uncountable nouns in general statements; talking about things in general
eg: Dogs are loyal animals
eg: Water is essential for life
eg: Children need love and attention
eg: Coffee keeps me awake

use: Proper nouns and names
eg: John is my brother
eg: London is a great city
eg: Japan has a rich culture
eg: Microsoft announced new products

use: Meals in general; unless referring to a specific meal
eg: Breakfast is the most important meal
eg: We had lunch at noon
eg: What's for dinner tonight?
eg: I'll see you at the dinner we're hosting (specific)

use: Sports and games
eg: I play football every weekend
eg: Do you want to play chess?
eg: She enjoys playing tennis

use: Languages
eg: She speaks English fluently
eg: I'm studying French
eg: Arabic is a beautiful language

use: Academic subjects
eg: I study math at university
eg: History is my favorite subject
eg: She teaches biology

use: Days and months
eg: I'll see you on Monday
eg: The meeting is in January
eg: We're leaving on Friday

use: Transportation with "by"
eg: I go to work by car
eg: They traveled by plane
eg: We came by bus

use: Certain places with their primary purpose; the activity, not the building
eg: Children go to school to learn
eg: It's time to go to bed
eg: She goes to work at 8 am
eg: They go to church every Sunday
eg: He was sent to prison for five years

tip: Compare "go to school" (as a student) vs "go to the school" (the building, perhaps to pick up a child)
tip: "in hospital" (British) / "in the hospital" (American) can differ; "in prison" = incarcerated vs "in the prison" = the building

# a / an vs. the — comparison

use: "a/an" = any one, first mention, unspecified; "the" = specific one, known, definite
eg: I need a doctor (any doctor will do)
eg: I need the doctor (our doctor, the one we know)

use: "a/an" = introducing; "the" = referring back
eg: She's reading a book (any book, we don't know which)
eg: She's reading the book (the specific book we've discussed)

use: "a/an" = one of many; "the" = the only one or the specific one
eg: There's a cat in the garden (one cat, indefinite)
eg: The cat is sleeping (our cat, or the one we know)

use: General vs specific
eg: A tiger is a dangerous animal (tigers in general)
eg: The tiger escaped from the zoo (that specific tiger)

use: Jobs with "a/an" vs unique roles with "the"
eg: She is a president of a company (one of many company presidents)
eg: She is the president of the company (the one and only)

# common mistakes with articles

use: Missing "a/an" before singular countable nouns
eg: Wrong: I have dog. Correct: I have a dog
eg: Wrong: She is teacher. Correct: She is a teacher
eg: Wrong: He gave me idea. Correct: He gave me an idea

use: Using "a/an" with uncountable nouns
eg: Wrong: I need an advice. Correct: I need advice
eg: Wrong: She has a furniture. Correct: She has furniture
eg: Wrong: Can you give me an information? Correct: Can you give me some information?

use: Using "a/an" with plural nouns
eg: Wrong: I saw a dogs. Correct: I saw some dogs or I saw dogs
eg: Wrong: She has a books. Correct: She has books

use: Using "the" when no article is needed (general statements)
eg: Wrong: The dogs are loyal animals. Correct: Dogs are loyal animals
eg: Wrong: The water is essential. Correct: Water is essential

use: Using "the" with proper nouns that don't take it
eg: Wrong: I visited the Paris. Correct: I visited Paris
eg: Wrong: She lives in the Japan. Correct: She lives in Japan

use: Using "the" incorrectly with meals, sports, languages
eg: Wrong: I have the breakfast at 8. Correct: I have breakfast at 8
eg: Wrong: I play the football. Correct: I play football
eg: Wrong: She speaks the English. Correct: She speaks English

use: Wrong choice between "a" and "an" based on spelling instead of sound
eg: Wrong: a hour. Correct: an hour (silent h, vowel sound)
eg: Wrong: an university. Correct: a university (/juː/ consonant sound)
eg: Wrong: a MBA. Correct: an MBA (em-bee-ay, vowel sound)

use: Using "the" with transportation by
eg: Wrong: I go by the car. Correct: I go by car
eg: Wrong: We traveled by the plane. Correct: We traveled by plane

use: Confusing "a little" vs "little" and "a few" vs "few"
eg: I have a little money (some, positive). I have little money (almost none, negative)
eg: I have a few friends (some). I have few friends (not many, negative)
