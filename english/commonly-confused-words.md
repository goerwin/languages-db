# accept / except

## accept

pho: /əkˈsɛpt/
def: To receive or agree to something willingly
eg: She accepted the job offer without hesitation
eg: Do you accept credit cards at this store?

## except

pho: /ɪkˈsɛpt/
def: Not including; other than
eg: Everyone passed the test except for Tom
eg: The store is open every day except Sunday

tip: "Accept" is a verb (to receive). "Except" is a preposition/conjunction (to exclude).

# adapt / adopt

## adapt

pho: /əˈdæpt/
def: To adjust or modify to fit new conditions
eg: It took him a while to adapt to the new school
eg: The movie was adapted from a bestselling novel

## adopt

pho: /əˈdɑpt/
def: To take up or choose; to legally take another's child as one's own
eg: The committee voted to adopt the new policy
eg: They decided to adopt a child from overseas

tip: "Adapt" means to change yourself. "Adopt" means to take something (an idea, a child) as your own.

# advice / advise

## advice

pho: /ədˈvaɪs/
def: A recommendation or opinion about what to do (noun)
eg: My father gave me good advice about saving money
eg: I need your advice on which car to buy

## advise

pho: /ədˈvaɪz/
def: To give recommendations or counsel (verb)
eg: I advise you to arrive early for the interview
eg: The doctor advised her to get more rest

tip: "Advice" is a noun (the thing given). "Advise" is a verb (the act of giving it). Same pattern as device/devise.

# affect / effect

## affect

pho: /əˈfɛkt/
def: To influence or have an impact on (verb)
eg: The bad news affected her mood for the rest of the day
eg: Smoking can seriously affect your health

## effect

pho: /ɪˈfɛkt/
def: A result or outcome (noun); to bring about (verb, less common)
eg: The medicine had a positive effect on his recovery
eg: The new law will take effect next month

tip: "Affect" is usually a verb (to influence). "Effect" is usually a noun (the result). Remember: Affect = Action, Effect = End result.

# all ready / already

## all ready

pho: /ɔl ˈrɛdi/
def: Everyone or everything is prepared
eg: The team was all ready to start the game
eg: Are you all ready for the trip?

## already

pho: /ɔlˈrɛdi/
def: By or before a specified time; so soon
eg: I've already finished my homework
eg: Is it noon already?

tip: "All ready" = fully prepared (two words). "Already" = by this time (one word, adverb).

# all together / altogether

## all together

pho: /ɔl təˈɡɛðər/
def: Everyone or everything in one place or at once
eg: The family was all together for the holidays
eg: Let's sing all together now

## altogether

pho: /ˌɔltəˈɡɛðər/
def: Completely; in total; on the whole
eg: That's altogether different from what I expected
eg: There were twelve people altogether

tip: "All together" = as a group (two words). "Altogether" = entirely or in total (one word).

# allusion / illusion

## allusion

pho: /əˈluʒən/
def: An indirect reference to something
eg: The poem contains an allusion to Greek mythology
eg: His speech included several allusions to the company's history

## illusion

pho: /ɪˈluʒən/
def: A false perception or deceptive appearance
eg: The magician created the illusion that the coin had vanished
eg: The mirror gives the illusion of more space

tip: "Allusion" = reference (think "allude"). "Illusion" = something that looks real but isn't (think "optical illusion").

# among / between

## among

pho: /əˈmʌŋ/
def: In the midst of; surrounded by (typically three or more)
eg: She felt comfortable among her friends
eg: Divide the candy among the children

## between

pho: /bɪˈtwin/
def: In the space separating (typically two things)
eg: The agreement was between the two companies
eg: Choose between the red and blue options

tip: "Among" is for three or more. "Between" is for two. (Though "between" is sometimes used for more when relationships are considered individually.)

# assure / ensure / insure

## assure

pho: /əˈʃʊr/
def: To tell someone confidently that something is true; to remove doubt
eg: I assure you that the report will be ready on time
eg: He assured his mother that he was safe

## ensure

pho: /ɪnˈʃʊr/
def: To make certain that something happens
eg: Please ensure that all doors are locked before leaving
eg: This policy ensures fair treatment for everyone

## insure

pho: /ɪnˈʃʊr/
def: To protect against loss with an insurance policy
eg: You should insure your car against theft
eg: We need to insure the house before the storm

tip: "Assure" = reassure a person. "Ensure" = make sure something happens. "Insure" = protect with insurance (financial).

# bare / bear

## bare

pho: /bɛr/
def: Uncovered; naked; minimal
eg: The trees were bare in winter
eg: She told the bare facts of the incident

## bear

pho: /bɛr/
def: To carry or support; to endure; the animal
eg: I can't bear the thought of losing you
eg: The bridge cannot bear that much weight

tip: "Bare" = uncovered (like bare feet). "Bear" = carry or endure (or the animal). Remember: bare = bald, bear = carry.

# beside / besides

## beside

pho: /bɪˈsaɪd/
def: Next to; at the side of
eg: Sit beside me on the couch
eg: The lamp is beside the bed

## besides

pho: /bɪˈsaɪdz/
def: In addition to; moreover; otherwise
eg: Besides English, she speaks Spanish and French
eg: I don't want to go; besides, I'm too tired

tip: "Beside" = next to (physical position). "Besides" = in addition (adds information).

# borrow / lend

## borrow

pho: /ˈbɑroʊ/
def: To take something temporarily with permission to return it
eg: Can I borrow your pen for a moment?
eg: She borrowed money from the bank

## lend

pho: /lɛnd/
def: To give something temporarily to someone
eg: I'll lend you my umbrella
eg: The library lends books to members

tip: You borrow FROM someone. You lend TO someone. The borrower receives; the lender gives.

# breath / breathe

## breath

pho: /brɛθ/
def: The air taken in or expelled from the lungs (noun)
eg: Take a deep breath and relax
eg: He was out of breath after running

## breathe

pho: /brið/
def: To take air into and expel from the lungs (verb)
eg: Breathe slowly and count to ten
eg: It's hard to breathe at high altitudes

tip: "Breath" is the noun (ends with -th, like "death"). "Breathe" is the verb (has an E, so you can "breathe easily").

# bring / take

## bring

pho: /brɪŋ/
def: To carry or convey toward the speaker
eg: Please bring your books to class tomorrow
eg: Don't forget to bring a gift to the party

## take

pho: /teɪk/
def: To carry or convey away from the speaker
eg: Take this package to the post office
eg: I'll take the kids to school this morning

tip: "Bring" = toward the speaker (bring it here). "Take" = away from the speaker (take it there).

# capital / capitol

## capital

pho: /ˈkæpɪtl/
def: A city that is the seat of government; money; uppercase letter; excellent
eg: Paris is the capital of France
eg: We need to raise more capital for the project

## capitol

pho: /ˈkæpɪtl/
def: The building where a legislature meets
eg: The senators gathered at the Capitol for the vote
eg: We toured the state capitol building

tip: "Capital" = city, money, or important. "Capitol" = the building (note the -ol ending, like the dome of a capitol building).

# cite / site / sight

## cite

pho: /saɪt/
def: To quote or refer to as evidence
eg: Be sure to cite your sources in the essay
eg: The lawyer cited several precedents

## site

pho: /saɪt/
def: A location or place
eg: This is the site of the ancient temple
eg: We found the perfect site for our new office

## sight

pho: /saɪt/
def: The ability to see; something seen; a view
eg: She lost her sight in the accident
eg: The Grand Canyon is an amazing sight

tip: "Cite" = quote (like citation). "Site" = location (like website). "Sight" = vision (like eyesight).

# complement / compliment

## complement

pho: /ˈkɑmpləmɛnt/
def: Something that completes or goes well with another
eg: Wine is a good complement to cheese
eg: The new software will complement our existing system

## compliment

pho: /ˈkɑmpləmɛnt/
def: A polite expression of praise or admiration
eg: She paid him a compliment on his presentation
eg: I complimented her on her new dress

tip: "Complement" = completes (both have E). "Compliment" = praise (both have I). Complement completes; compliment is nice.

# conscience / conscious

## conscience

pho: /ˈkɑnʃəns/
def: The moral sense of right and wrong
eg: His conscience wouldn't let him lie
eg: I have a clear conscience about my decision

## conscious

pho: /ˈkɑnʃəs/
def: Aware of one's surroundings; awake; deliberate
eg: She was conscious of being watched
eg: He made a conscious effort to be polite

tip: "Conscience" = your inner moral guide (noun). "Conscious" = aware or awake (adjective).

# council / counsel

## council

pho: /ˈkaʊnsəl/
def: A group of people who govern or advise
eg: The city council voted on the new ordinance
eg: She was elected to the student council

## counsel

pho: /ˈkaʊnsəl/
def: Advice or guidance; a lawyer; to give advice
eg: I sought counsel from my mentor
eg: The defendant has the right to legal counsel

tip: "Council" = a group/committee (like city council). "Counsel" = advice or advisor (like legal counsel).

# desert / dessert

## desert

pho: /ˈdɛzərt/
def: A dry, sandy region with little vegetation; to abandon
eg: The Sahara is the world's largest desert
eg: He would never desert his friends in need

## dessert

pho: /dɪˈzɜrt/
def: A sweet course served at the end of a meal
eg: What's for dessert tonight?
eg: I'll have the chocolate cake for dessert

tip: "Desert" has one S (like sand). "Dessert" has two S's (you want seconds of dessert).

# disinterested / uninterested

## disinterested

pho: /dɪsˈɪntrəstɪd/
def: Impartial; having no stake in the outcome
eg: We need a disinterested party to mediate the dispute
eg: The judge must remain disinterested

## uninterested

pho: /ʌnˈɪntrəstɪd/
def: Not interested; indifferent; bored
eg: He seemed uninterested in the conversation
eg: She was completely uninterested in sports

tip: "Disinterested" = impartial (fair, no bias). "Uninterested" = not interested (bored, indifferent).

# elicit / illicit

## elicit

pho: /ɪˈlɪsɪt/
def: To draw out or evoke a response
eg: The teacher tried to elicit answers from the students
eg: His comment elicited laughter from the crowd

## illicit

pho: /ɪˈlɪsɪt/
def: Illegal; forbidden; not permitted
eg: The police uncovered illicit drug activity
eg: They had an illicit affair

tip: "Elicit" = draw out (verb, like extract). "Illicit" = illegal (adjective, like illicit drugs).

# emigrate / immigrate

## emigrate

pho: /ˈɛmɪˌɡreɪt/
def: To leave one's country to live elsewhere
eg: Her family emigrated from Poland in the 1950s
eg: He plans to emigrate to Canada

## immigrate

pho: /ˈɪmɪˌɡreɪt/
def: To enter and settle in a new country
eg: They immigrated to the United States last year
eg: Many people immigrate in search of better opportunities

tip: "Emigrate" = EXIT your country (E for exit). "Immigrate" = come IN to a new country (I for in).

# empathy / sympathy

## empathy

pho: /ˈɛmpəθi/
def: The ability to understand and share another's feelings
eg: She showed great empathy for the grieving family
eg: Good teachers have empathy for their students

## sympathy

pho: /ˈsɪmpəθi/
def: Feelings of pity or sorrow for someone's misfortune
eg: We extend our sympathy to the bereaved family
eg: I have no sympathy for people who cheat

tip: "Empathy" = feeling WITH someone (understanding their experience). "Sympathy" = feeling FOR someone (compassion from outside).

# envy / jealousy

## envy

pho: /ˈɛnvi/
def: Desire for what someone else has
eg: I envy her ability to speak three languages
eg: His success aroused envy in his colleagues

## jealousy

pho: /ˈdʒɛləsi/
def: Fear of losing something (especially a relationship) to a rival
eg: His jealousy destroyed their relationship
eg: She felt jealousy when he talked to other women

tip: "Envy" = wanting what others have. "Jealousy" = fear of losing what you have (often in relationships).

# everyday / every day

## everyday

pho: /ˈɛvriˌdeɪ/
def: Ordinary; commonplace; used routinely (adjective)
eg: These are my everyday shoes
eg: It's just an everyday occurrence

## every day

pho: /ˈɛvri ˈdeɪ/
def: Each day; daily (adverbial phrase)
eg: I exercise every day
eg: She reads the newspaper every day

tip: "Everyday" = ordinary (one word, adjective). "Every day" = each day (two words, when/how often).

# farther / further

## farther

pho: /ˈfɑrðər/
def: At or to a greater physical distance
eg: The store is farther than I thought
eg: We walked farther than planned

## further

pho: /ˈfɜrðər/
def: To a greater degree or extent; additional
eg: We need to investigate this further
eg: Do you have any further questions?

tip: "Farther" = physical distance (far). "Further" = metaphorical distance or degree (further discussion, further evidence).

# fewer / less

## fewer

pho: /ˈfjuər/
def: A smaller number of (used with countable nouns)
eg: Fewer people attended this year
eg: I have fewer books than you do

## less

pho: /lɛs/
def: A smaller amount of (used with uncountable nouns)
eg: I need less sugar in my coffee
eg: We have less time than we thought

tip: "Fewer" = count nouns (fewer apples, fewer mistakes). "Less" = mass nouns (less water, less trouble).

# good / well

## good

pho: /ɡʊd/
def: Of high quality; morally right; suitable (adjective)
eg: That was a good movie
eg: She is a good friend

## well

pho: /wɛl/
def: In a satisfactory manner; in good health (adverb when modifying verbs)
eg: She sings well
eg: I don't feel well today

tip: "Good" is an adjective (modifies nouns). "Well" is an adverb (modifies verbs). You do something WELL; something IS good.

# hanged / hung

## hanged

pho: /hæŋd/
def: Executed by suspension from the neck (past tense of hang, for people)
eg: The prisoner was hanged at dawn
eg: They hanged the convicted murderer

## hung

pho: /hʌŋ/
def: Suspended or attached (past tense of hang, for objects)
eg: She hung the picture on the wall
eg: The laundry was hung out to dry

tip: "Hanged" = executed (people only). "Hung" = suspended (objects). People are hanged; pictures are hung.

# historic / historical

## historic

pho: /hɪˈstɔrɪk/
def: Famous or important in history; momentous
eg: This is a historic moment for our nation
eg: They signed the historic peace treaty

## historical

pho: /hɪˈstɔrɪkəl/
def: Relating to or concerning history; from the past
eg: The novel is set in a historical period
eg: We need to consider the historical context

tip: "Historic" = significant, memorable. "Historical" = relating to history (factual, from the past).

# imply / infer

## imply

pho: /ɪmˈplaɪ/
def: To suggest or hint without stating directly (speaker/writer does this)
eg: His tone implied that he was angry
eg: Are you implying that I'm lying?

## infer

pho: /ɪnˈfɜr/
def: To conclude from evidence or reasoning (listener/reader does this)
eg: From his expression, I inferred that he disagreed
eg: What can we infer from these results?

tip: Speakers/writers IMPLY. Listeners/readers INFER. You imply something; I infer it.

# its / it's

## its

pho: /ɪts/
def: Belonging to it (possessive pronoun)
eg: The dog wagged its tail
eg: The company announced its new product

## it's

pho: /ɪts/
def: It is or it has (contraction)
eg: It's a beautiful day
eg: It's been a long time since we met

tip: "Its" = possessive (like his, hers). "It's" = it is or it has. If you can say "it is," use "it's."

# lay / lie

## lay

pho: /leɪ/
def: To put or place something down (transitive; needs an object)
eg: Lay the book on the table
eg: She laid the baby in the crib

## lie

pho: /laɪ/
def: To recline or rest (intransitive; no object)
eg: I need to lie down for a while
eg: The dog lies on the floor

tip: "Lay" = put something down (lay the book). "Lie" = recline (lie down). Lay takes an object; lie doesn't. Past tense: lay/laid, lie/lay.

# lead / led

## lead

pho: /lid/ or /lɛd/
def: To guide or be first (verb, /lid/); the metal (noun, /lɛd/)
eg: She will lead the team to victory
eg: Lead pipes can be dangerous

## led

pho: /lɛd/
def: Past tense of lead (to guide)
eg: He led the expedition through the mountains
eg: The path led us to a hidden cave

tip: "Lead" (verb) = guide, pronounced /lid/. "Led" = past tense of lead. "Lead" (metal) = /lɛd/, same spelling as led but different meaning.

# loose / lose

## loose

pho: /lus/
def: Not tight; not fastened; free from restraint
eg: My pants are too loose
eg: The dog got loose from its leash

## lose

pho: /luz/
def: To be deprived of; to fail to win
eg: Don't lose your keys
eg: I hope we don't lose the game

tip: "Loose" = not tight (loose = loose). "Lose" = misplace or fail (lose the 'o' when you lose something).

# passed / past

## passed

pho: /pæst/
def: Past tense of pass (to go by, succeed, or transfer)
eg: She passed the exam with flying colors
eg: A truck passed us on the highway

## past

pho: /pæst/
def: Gone by; previous time; beyond (noun, adjective, preposition, adverb)
eg: In the past, things were different
eg: He walked past the store

tip: "Passed" is always a verb (past tense of pass). "Past" is never a verb—it's a noun, adjective, or preposition.

# personal / personnel

## personal

pho: /ˈpɜrsənəl/
def: Belonging to or affecting a particular person; private
eg: This is a personal matter
eg: She shared her personal experience

## personnel

pho: /ˌpɜrsəˈnɛl/
def: Employees or staff of an organization
eg: All personnel must attend the meeting
eg: The personnel department handles hiring

tip: "Personal" = private, individual (personal belongings). "Personnel" = employees (personnel department = HR).

## proceed

pho: /proʊˈsid/
def: To continue or go forward
eg: Please proceed with your presentation
eg: We will proceed to the next item on the agenda

tip: "Precede" = come before (pre- = before). "Proceed" = go forward (pro- = forward).

# principal / principle

eg: The principal addressed the students

## principle

pho: /ˈprɪnsəpəl/
def: A fundamental truth, rule, or belief
eg: She stands by her principles
eg: The principle of equality is important

tip: "Principal" = main or school head (your "pal" the principal). "Principle" = rule or belief (ends in -le like rule).

# raise / rise

## raise

pho: /reɪz/
def: To lift up; to increase; to bring up (transitive; needs an object)
eg: Raise your hand if you have a question
eg: They raised three children

## rise

pho: /raɪz/
def: To move upward; to get up (intransitive; no object)
eg: The sun rises in the east
eg: Prices continue to rise

tip: "Raise" takes an object (raise something). "Rise" does not (something rises). You raise your hand; the sun rises.

# sensible / sensitive

eg: That was a sensible decision

## sensitive

eg: She is very sensitive to criticism
eg: His skin is sensitive to sunlight

tip: "Sensible" = practical, wise. "Sensitive" = easily affected, delicate. A sensible person makes good choices; a sensitive person feels deeply.

## stationery

eg: I need to buy some stationery for thank-you notes

tip: "Stationary" = staying in place (a for anchor). "Stationery" = paper for writing (e for envelope).

# than / then

## than

pho: /ðæn/

eg: I would rather walk than drive

## then

pho: /ðɛn/
def: At that time; next; in that case
eg: We lived in Boston then
eg: First mix the ingredients, then bake

tip: "Than" = comparison (bigger than, rather than). "Then" = time or sequence (and then, back then).

# their / there / they're

## their

pho: /ðɛr/
def: Belonging to them (possessive)
eg: The students left their books in the classroom
eg: Their house is on the corner

## there

pho: /ðɛr/
def: In or at that place; used to introduce a sentence
eg: Put the box over there
eg: There are many reasons to be grateful

## they're

pho: /ðɛr/
def: They are (contraction)
eg: They're going to the party tonight
eg: I think they're late

tip: "Their" = possessive (their car). "There" = place (over there) or existence (there is). "They're" = they are.

# to / too / two

## to

pho: /tu/ or /tə/
def: Toward; until; used before a verb (infinitive)
eg: I'm going to the store
eg: She wants to learn Spanish

## too

pho: /tu/
def: Also; excessively
eg: I want to come too
eg: This coffee is too hot

## two

pho: /tu/
def: The number 2
eg: I have two cats
eg: We'll meet in two hours

tip: "To" = direction or infinitive. "Too" = also or excessively (has an extra O). "Two" = the number 2 (has a W for two).

# weather / whether

## weather

pho: /ˈwɛðər/
def: The state of the atmosphere (rain, sun, etc.)
eg: The weather is nice today
eg: We can't control the weather

## whether

pho: /ˈwɛðər/
def: Expressing a choice or doubt between alternatives
eg: I don't know whether to go or stay
eg: It depends on whether she agrees

tip: "Weather" = rain, sun, temperature. "Whether" = if, expressing alternatives. Weather has "wea" (we experience it).

# who / whom

## who

pho: /hu/
def: Used as subject (does the action)
eg: Who is calling?
eg: The person who called left a message

## whom

pho: /hum/
def: Used as object (receives the action)
eg: To whom should I address this letter?
eg: The woman whom you met is my sister

tip: "Who" = subject (who did it?). "Whom" = object (to whom? by whom?). If you'd use "he/she," use "who"; if "him/her," use "whom."

# who's / whose

## who's

pho: /huz/
def: Who is or who has (contraction)
eg: Who's coming to the party?
eg: Who's been using my computer?

## whose

pho: /huz/
def: Belonging to whom (possessive)
eg: Whose jacket is this?
eg: The author whose book won the prize

tip: "Who's" = who is or who has. "Whose" = belonging to whom. If you can say "who is," use "who's."

# your / you're

## your

pho: /jʊr/ or /jɔr/
def: Belonging to you (possessive)
eg: Is this your book?
eg: Your idea is excellent

## you're

pho: /jʊr/ or /jɔr/
def: You are (contraction)
eg: You're going to love this movie
eg: I think you're right

tip: "Your" = possessive (your car). "You're" = you are. If you can say "you are," use "you're."
