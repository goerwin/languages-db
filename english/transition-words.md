# a case in point

cat: example
def: Used to introduce a specific example that illustrates the point being made
eg: Many countries have reduced emissions. A case in point is Sweden, which cut carbon by 25% in a decade
eg: He's always late. A case in point: he missed the meeting again yesterday
pos: start, middle

# above all

cat: emphasis
def: Used to indicate the most important point among several; emphasizes priority
eg: We need to be efficient, professional, and above all, honest with our clients
eg: Above all, remember to stay calm in emergencies
pos: start, middle

# accordingly

cat: result
def: Used to indicate that an action follows logically from what was just stated
eg: The budget was approved. Accordingly, we can proceed with the project
eg: Sales have dropped; accordingly, we need to revise our strategy
pos: start, middle

# admittedly

cat: concession
def: Used to acknowledge that something is true, even if it weakens your argument
eg: Admittedly, the plan has some flaws, but it's the best option we have
eg: The food was, admittedly, not the best I've ever had
pos: start, middle

# all in all

cat: conclusion
def: Used to give a final assessment after considering everything
eg: All in all, it was a successful conference despite the technical issues
eg: All in all, I think we made the right decision
pos: start, middle

# all the same

cat: concession
def: Used to introduce a contrasting point despite what was just said; similar to "nevertheless"
eg: The weather wasn't great. All the same, we had a wonderful trip
eg: I understand your concerns. All the same, I think we should proceed
pos: start, middle

# all things considered

cat: generalization
def: Used when making a judgment after taking all factors into account
eg: All things considered, she's the best candidate for the job
eg: All things considered, we got off lightly
pos: start, middle

# along the same lines

cat: comparison
def: Used to introduce something similar to what was just discussed
eg: We could offer a discount. Along the same lines, we might consider free shipping
eg: His proposal was good. Along the same lines, I'd suggest we also look at marketing
pos: start, middle

# along with

cat: addition
def: Used to add another item or person to a list
eg: The report, along with the supporting documents, will be sent tomorrow
eg: She brought her laptop along with her notes
pos: middle

# apart from that

cat: addition
def: Used to add another point or exception to what was said
eg: The venue was perfect. Apart from that, the catering could have been better
eg: Apart from that one issue, everything went smoothly
pos: start, middle

# as a consequence

cat: result
def: Used to introduce the result or effect of something previously mentioned
eg: The factory closed. As a consequence, hundreds lost their jobs
eg: He missed the deadline; as a consequence, the project was delayed

# as a matter of fact

cat: emphasis
def: Used to add emphasis to a statement or to correct a misconception; similar to "actually" but stronger
eg: I'm not tired at all. As a matter of fact, I could keep working for hours
eg: As a matter of fact, she was the one who suggested the idea in the first place
pos: start, middle

# as a result

cat: result
def: Used to introduce the outcome or effect of a previous action or situation
eg: The roads were icy. As a result, many flights were cancelled
eg: He studied hard; as a result, he passed the exam with flying colors

# as a rule
